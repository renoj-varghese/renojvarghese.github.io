Subconscious: Mental Health Storytelling Platform Redesign

introduction

Subsconsious is a non-profit dedicated to telling real people's mental health stories. My mission was redesigning the initial experience readers have with Subconscious's web platform. The first touchpoint is important to convince the reader the value of mental health storytelling.

Goal 1

Simplify finding mental health stories and resources.

Goal 2

Introduce the Subconscious organization, its mission, and history.

Goal 3

Showcase how donations support Subconcious's mission.

Overview

Role: UX Researcher, UX Designer

Team: The Subconscious Team: Cathy Liu (Operations), Allison Chang (Operations), Robin Kuang (Design Mentor), and Jennifer Chiang (Content)

Contributions: User Testing, Personas, Journey Maps, Final Redesigns

Duration: 10 Weeks

research

Research on UserTesting.com

The first step in the process was conducting user tests with real people. My main tool was UserTesting.com that provided video and audio recordings of people exploring the Subconscious website.

Key Insights and Opportunities

Finding a story is challenging without navigation or search tools

Let's make mental health stories easier to find

People want to learn more about the Subconscious organization

There's an opportunity to build trust by sharing more information about Subconscious

Readers are hesistant to donate because they are unsure where Donations go

One opportunity is to redesign how donations are presented

Personas and Journey Maps

Personas and Journey Maps helped summarize my research results to the Subconscious organization. With these, I was able to have a conversation with organization leaders on how we could better introduce Subconscious to new readers. The documents guided my designs as I thought about how I could improve Jamie's and Tara's experiences on the Subconscious website.

Frequent Stakeholder Checkins

A key element in the design and research process was frequently meeting with members of the operations, design, and content teams. Their feedback helped me understand the aspirations for Subconscious and incorporate the team's ideas in the final redesign.

design

Sketches and Concepts

The next step was to hit the ground running and think of solutions. The best tool for this was paper and pen to explore many ideas in a short amount of time.

High Fidelity Prototype

Since there was an already established design system, I could easily convert my sketches into a high fidelity prototype. With 2-3 design iterations and frequent checkins with stakeholders, I developed the following solutions.

Designing the Homepage to Make Stories Easier to Find

Problem: When asked to find a specific story participants took 2-4 minutes to find. 2/7 participants were unable to find the story at all.

Solution: Adding a search and filter tool on the homepage could help readers find stories.

Search and Filter goes a step further than a simple search bar to show what readers can find

Telling Subconscious's Story To Increase Clarity

Problem: 6/7 participants had questions about how Subconscious started, who the team was, and why the organization was created.

Solution: Include more information on Subconscious's history. Use headers to help readers scan the information they are looking for.

Present Subconscious's history in both a readable and visual format
Learn about the organization founders and their mental health stories

Presenting Donations with Detail and Transparency

Problem: 4/7 participants who reported that they would be unlikely to donate were also unsure about how their donation would be used.

Solution: Update the Donations page with clear categories that help showcase transparency and clarity about donation funds.

Redesign openly breaks down expenses from previous fiscal year

A/B Testing

With the redesign ready, it was time to test. Using UserTesting.com again, I conducted two sets of user tests focusing on how we could present the Donations page. One prototype contained a Donations page with explicit percentages and another with imagery.

Version A Same test, except donations are stated with explicit percentages.

Version B Same test, except donations are represented by graphical icons.

Testing Results

Readers were as likely to donate with version A as with version B

There were no significant differences between how readers responded to either versions of the donation page

Participants reported they were 40% more likely to donate compared to the original site

For both the original site and the redesign participants were asked to rate how likely they would be to donate to Subconscious. The average ratings for the redesign was 40% higher than for the original site.

No issues found finding information about donations or about the Subconscious organization

The participants in the redesign reported no problems finding information throughout the site.

conclusion

Summary

  • Told the story of Subconscious, a mission driven non-profit dedicated to people's mental health stories.
  • Solved navigation issues to find mental health stories through search tools
  • Developed the About Us page to show readers how the organization started
  • Increased clarity and transparency about donations and Subconscious.

Challenges and Lessons

CopyWriting

When redesigning the site, I focused more on layout and hierarchy, using dummy text for copy. But to test with users, I needed more realistic copy for readers to get an understanding of who Subconscious is. The Operations and Content teams volunteered to do copywriting, while I could focus on the design.
