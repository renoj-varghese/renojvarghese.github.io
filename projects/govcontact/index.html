GovContact

introduction

Some of the challenges of contacting a legislator are (1) learning about representatives, (2) staying up to date with representatives' actions, and (3) sending frequent messages. Contacting your legislator is one of the most effective ways to use your political voice.

GovContact is a mobile app that makes contacting a representative a simpler process.

Goal 1

Inform citizens about representatives' contact info and political stance.

Goal 2

Keep citizens up to date with representatives’ actions and policies.

Goal 3

Encourage citizens to send frequent, clear, and concise messages.

research

Focus Group

Hitting the ground running, I arranged a focus group with citizens to receive a large amount of qualitative insights in a short amount of time. Questions and discussion focused on how citizens would contact reps.

  • "It would be nice to have all my representatives' information in one place on my phone ... like having my Senator's phone number in my Contacts" – Emily

Online Research

To understand civic engagement beyond my focus group and interviews, I found research online that revealed new insights into the problem.

  • 70% of communication staffers ranked personalized messages (emails, phone calls, letters) as "Very Important" towards understanding constituents opinions.– Congress Management Foundation

Interviews with Rep Offices

After the focus group, I wanted to understand how a message reaches a rep. I contacted my own reps' offices and conducted surveys on how a citizen could best get their voice heard.

  • Representative employees answer citizen reports rather than representatives themselves. Employees forward important messages and create summary reports.

Competitive Analysis

Finally, analysis into civic apps provided inspiration into what works and what could be improved.

Takeaways

  • Open States and Capitol Call allow citizens to see current bills on the docket. Knowing the bills a representative has written says a lot about what the representative supports.
  • Some apps, like Brigade and Capitol Call, had a complicated onboarding process, which included a long sign-up process, surveys, or account verifications.

Key Insights and Opportunities

The focus group showed how citizens would spend most of their time researching who their representatives are

Goal 1: One opportunity is to provide a citizens' reps and their contact info in one place

One useful feature found in the Open States and Capitol Call was to see bills that representatives had written

Goal 2: There's an opporunity to expand this idea to help citizens stay updated with all of a representative's activities

Talking to government offices and conducting online research showed that frequent communication is important to affect real change

design

Design Evolution

There were 4 iterations of design and testing to get towards the final solution. At each stage, I incorporated feedback from users and mentors. Testing consisted of citizens using a prototype in an interview format and answering followup questions. Mentors provided more formal critique.

Representative Cards

Cycle through individual cards to meet reps and learn how to contact them.

Rationale

Based on the focus group, the main problem to solve was consoldiating representatives’ info in one place.

Feedback

If only contact info is present, it’s nicer to have them laid out one screen rather than cycling through one by one.

0% of participants thought they would need more contact information (office address, rss feed)

Single Page Layout

See representative information on a single screen. Easy to scroll through and meet all representatives.

Rationale

Feedback from first user test showed that citizens wanted to be able to meet their representatives faster.

Focus Group and Online Research demonstrated how citizens should know where representatives stand on the issues.

Feedback

Citizens needed more information on representatives’ political stance.

Representative Profiles

Profiles contain both contact info and individualized information about political stance.

Rationale

Previous user test showed citizens needed to know more about a political stance than political party. Rep Profiles ignore political party and look at how each rep has supported the issues.

Pulled away from a single page layout to chunk more appropriate information.

Feedback

Citizens would want to stay updated with what representatives are up to on a daily basis as well as message reps inside GovContact.

Adding Political Feed and Messaging

Political Feed scrapes social media, petitions, and legislation to inform and prompt citizens to act. Citizens can use in-app messaging to send text, voice, and video.

Rationale

Expanded feature from Open States and Capitol Call to see representatives' bills to include all of their activities.

Online Research and Focus Group showed that citizens need to trust information source. Political Feed relies on primary sources rather than news outlets.

Previous user test showed that citizens would also want to message reps within the app.

Feedback

Engagements from social media, petitions, and bills could be sent to reps as a summary report to be used to understand citizens’ opinions.

Challenges and Lessons

Incorporating Different Forms of Research

This was one of the most research-heavy projects I encountered. Civic engagement is a complicated issue that I had to learn about before I could start designing for it. The most effective forms of research were the focus group and government employee interviews. Talking to real people helped me develop a greater sense of empathy of what people needed in a civic engagment app.

Consider Visual Design Early On

To develop a richer visual language, I started the process early. The goal was to make each design choice have a meaning, which would require a longer period of time to develop.

Next Steps

Compare Reps Across Issues

Instead of learning about representatives individually, an interesting feature would be to compare all your representative stances based on a political issue.

Message History

One the features that would be needed is an inbox feature that citizens could use to keep track of where their messages ended up and if they received a response. Currently GovContact would send an email to citizens that they could continue further correspondence.
